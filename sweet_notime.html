Blueberry and Banana Breakfast Bars

For those with little time in the morning, pre-planning is a must. And these breakfast bars are not only easy to make ahead of time, but are healthy and filling to keep you energized throughout your busiest days. This recipe also yields enough servings to keep you prepped for days at a time.

Photo by Hayley Maxwell from Unsplash. Recipe adapted from Allrecipes.com.

  • Prep time: 15 minutes
  • Cook time: 35 minutes
  • Yields: 9 servings

  • 1 1/3 cup rolled oats
  • 1/3 cup coarsely chopped dried figs
  • 1/4 cup coarsely chopped almonds
  • 1/4 cup sunflower seeds
  • 1 cup of mashed ripe bananas
  • 1/3 cup smooth peanut butter
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 2 large egg whites
  • 2/3 cup fresh blueberries
  • 1 tablespoon flax seeds
  • 1 tablespoon sunflower seeds

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees farenheit.
  2. Line an 8-inch square baking pan with parchment paper, leaving 1 inch of parchment hanging over the sides.
  3. Mix oats, figs, almonds, and 1/4 cup sunflower seeds together in a bowl.
  4. Stir bananas, peanut butter, and honey together in a saucepan over medium-low heat until completely melted and smooth, about 5 minutes. Remove saucepan from heat.
  5. Whisk egg whites together in a bowl until foamy. Mix banana mixture into egg whites and whisk until smooth; add oat mixture and stir until batter well combined. Fold blueberries into batter.
  6. Press batter into the prepared pan; top with flax seeds and 1 tablespoon sunflower seeds.
  7. Bake in the preheated oven until golden, about 30 minutes.
  8. Cool completely before cutting into bars.
