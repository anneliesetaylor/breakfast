Berry Cheesecake French Toast

So decadent and rich it should almost be considered dessert, this berry cheesecake french toast is sure to turn your everyday morning meal into a special event. Perfect for brunch or even just to add some excitement to the weekly Saturday family breakfast. Customize with berries of your liking, or whatever is in season!

Photo by Toa Heftiba from Unsplash. Recipe adapted from Allrecipes.com.

  • Prep time: 20 minutes
  • Cook time: 20 minutes
  • Yields: 8 servings

  • 1 cup mashed fresh strawberries (or other berries of your choosing)
  • 1/2 cup white sugar
  • 1 cup of water
  • 1 cup of milk
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 6 eggs
  • 1 (8 ounce) package of cream cheese, softened
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 3/4 cup white sugar
  • 8 slices of bread, cut in half diagonally
  • 1 teaspoon of butter
  • 8 sliced fresh strawberries, or 1 cup of other seasonal berries
  • Confectioners' sugar for dusting, if desired
  • 1 cup whipped cream

  1. Preheat oven to 100 degree farenheit.
  2. Add mashed strawberries and 1/2 cup of sugar to a saucepan and warm over medium heat.
  3. Mix cornstarch and water together in a bowl before stirring the mixture into the pot with the mashed strawberries.
  4. Continue to cook and stir until the mixture thickens (about 5 minutes). Reduce the heat to low and leave to simmer while preparing remaining ingredients. Stir occasionally.
  5. Whisk together the milk and eggs in a bowl. Set aside.
  6. Mash cream cheese, vanilla extract, and 3/4 cup of sugar in a bowl until mixture reaches a smooth consistency.
  7. Spread a thick layer of the mixture over a half-slice of bread, and then top with another slice. Repeat with the remaining slices of bread and cream cheese mixture to make 8 triangle-shaped sandwiches.
  8. Heat butter in a large skillet over medium heat.
  9. Dip each sandwich into the egg mixture and place into the skillet.
  10. Cook until golden brown on both sides, approximately 3 minutes per side.
  11. Optional: Transfer pan-fried sandwiches to a baking sheet and place in the preheated oven to keep warm while cooking remaining slices.
  12. To serve, top with the berry compote prepared earlier, and add fresh fruit and confectioners' sugar if desired. Pair with a dollop of whipped cream.
