Vegan Crepes

Using less than ten ingredients and only requiring 20 mintues of cooking, these vegan crepes might just become your new go to breakfast dish. Versatile and easy to make, they're ready to be topped with whatever your heart desires.

Photo by Monika Grabkowska from Unsplash. Recipe adapted from Allrecipes.com.

  • Prep time: 5 minutes
  • Cook time: 20 minutes
  • Additional preparation: 2 hours
  • Yields: 4 servings

  • 1/2 cup soy milk
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/4 cup of melted soy margarine
  • 1 tablespoon turbinado sugar
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup
  • 1 cup unbleached all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

  1. In a large mixing bowl, mix soy milk, water, 1/4 cup margarine, sugar, syrup, flour, and salt together.
  2. Cover and let the mixture chill for about 2 hours.
  3. Lightly grease a 5-6 inch skillet with some soy margarine before heating the skillet until it's hot.
  4. Pour roughly 3 tablespoons of batter into the skillet. Swirl the pan to make the batter evenly coat the bottom of the skillet.
  5. Cook until golden brown, then flip and cook the opposite side.
  6. Top with syrup or honey and toppings of your choosing.

Tofu and Broccoli Quiche

Though slightly time intensive, this tofu and broccoli quiche is sure to be a crowd pleaser. Yeilding 6 servings, this dish is the perfect for your next brunch get-together (or, you can just keep the leftovers all to yourself).

Photo by Ang√®le Kamp from Unsplash. Recipe adapted from Allrecipes.com.

  • Prep time: 20 minutes
  • Cook time: 40 minutes
  • Additional preparation: 5 minutes
  • Yields: 6 servings

  • 1 (9 inch) unbaked pie crust
  • 1 pound broccoli, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 pound of firm tofu, drained
  • 1/2 cup of soy milk
  • 1/4 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 3/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground red pepper
  • 1 tablespoon dried parsley
  • black pepper to taste
  • 1/8 cup of Parmesan flavored soy cheese

  1. Preheat oven to 400 degrees farenheit. Bake pie crust in preheated oven for 10-12 minutes.
  2. Place chopped broccoli in a steamer over an inch of boiling water and cover the pot. Cook until tender, but still firm, for about 2 to 6 minutes.
  3. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Saute onion and garlic until golden before stirring in the cooked broccoli. Heat everything thoroughly.
  4. In a blender, combine tofu, soy milk, mustard, salt, nutmeg, ground red pepper, black pepper, parsley, and Parmesan soy cheese. Blend ingredients until mixture is smooth.
  5. In a large bowl, combine tofu mixture with broccoli mixture. Pour combined mixture into the pie crust.
  6. Bake in preheated oven until quiche becomes set, approximately 35-40 minutes. Let quiche stand for 5-10 minutes before cutting.
